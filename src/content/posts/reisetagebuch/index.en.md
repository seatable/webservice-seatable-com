## Why keep a travel diary?

If you have ever scrolled through old photos years after a trip, you know the problem: you might still remember the most spectacular sights, but the equally beautiful little moments have often disappeared. Where exactly was that sunset? What was the name of that little café? How did I feel on that hike? A travel diary helps you to **preserve exactly these memories permanently**. It documents not only places and information, but also feelings, encounters and spontaneous adventures that make your trip unique.

![Mountain lake: processing experiences in the travel diary](travel-diary-mountain-lake.jpg)

In addition, it can help you to **process your experiences** while still on the trip. If you write in your travel diary every evening, you automatically reflect on the day. This brings order to your impressions, emotions and thoughts, gets them out of your head more quickly and allows you to start the next day with a light heart. Many globetrotters even report that they **perceive moments more consciously** and thus experience their travels more intensely.

### The most important benefits at a glance

*   **Capture memories**: Record the emotional highlights of the day, breathtaking places and special encounters that would otherwise be forgotten.   
*   **Perfectly organized**: Save restaurant recommendations, sights, routes and personal insider tips for your next visit.
*   **Combine text, images and data**: Link photos, appointments and geodata with your diary entries – all in one central place.
*   **Template for photo books**: If you create a travel documentation, you can arrange photos more easily later and enrich them with context.

![Photos for the travel diary](travel-diary-photos.jpg)

## Digital travel diary vs. analog travel diary

There are basically two ways in which you can keep your travel diary. While some prefer a classic travel diary to fill in, others rely on digital solutions such as a travel diary app or an online travel diary.

### Analog travel diaries

If you write in your travel diary by hand, glue in postcards and tickets or add sketches, personal travel stories emerge over time that tell far more than a digital photo collection. So-called **scrapbooking**, where you design your travel diary with artistic elements, offers a lot of creative freedom here.

#### Advantages

*   No internet/Wi-Fi and no mobile devices with a charged battery required
*   Plenty of room for creativity thanks to hands-on book design on paper
*   Particularly personal character (through handwriting, style, souvenirs, etc.)
*   Digital detox without distractions

#### Disadvantages

*   Subsequent changes are cumbersome and unsightly
*   In case of loss or damage, all diary entries are lost
*   Photos have to be printed out and glued in later
*   More weight and less space in your luggage
    
![Woman designing an analog travel diary](travel-diary-handwritten.jpg)

### Digital travel diary

A digital travel diary enriches classic diary entries with additional information, media and functions. For example, add GPS data of places, upload photos and videos or save audio/voice recordings. If you create a careful digital travel documentation, you will benefit from the better overview **especially with a busy travel program involving a lot of data**.

#### Advantages

*   Multimedia design with photos, videos, links, etc.
*   Protection against loss thanks to secure storage in the [cloud]({{< relref "posts/cloud-computing" >}})
*   Entries can be edited at any time without visible changes
*   Flexible search, sort and filter functions
*   Easy sharing with family and friends

#### Disadvantages

*   Dependence on internet connections and charged batteries
*   Less DIY feeling, as no physical result is created
*   Less creativity due to the ready-made structure of apps and templates
*   Less concentration and more distraction from other device functions

## Ideas and tips for a travel diary to fill in

When you write in your travel diary, you probably first think of the day's experiences. But there are far more **travel diary ideas**. For example, consider what you particularly liked, what surprised you or challenged you. As a template for your travel diary ideas, you could use these thematic sections:

*   Highlights of the day
*   Weather and mood
*   Local cuisine
*   Personal ratings
*   Encounters with locals
*   Funny mishaps
*   New words in a foreign language
*   Favorite photo of the day
*   To-do list for the next day

![Tropical beach: sharpening sensory perception for the travel diary](travel-diary-tropical-beach.jpg)

{{< warning headline="Tip: Synesthesia" >}}

To capture moods better, you can pause for a moment and consciously notice which of your **senses** are activated at the same time right now. If you describe, for example, the sound of the sea, the warm sun rays on your skin, the salty air and the refreshing vanilla ice cream on your tongue, the memory becomes much more vivid than if you only write that you were at the beach.

{{< /warning >}}

To keep track, it is advisable to **add the respective date to your diary entries or write them chronologically into a calendar**. Nevertheless, you do not have to work through everything systematically every day. Focus on the moments and details that you really want to remember – and stay relaxed if gaps appear or you simply do not feel like writing in your travel diary. You can complete it later or simply leave out days. 

**Generally speaking, you cannot go wrong with a travel diary. The main thing is that it brings you joy!**

### Creative travel diary ideas

Anyone who enjoys creative work will find the ideal holiday activity in a personalized travel diary to fill in. **Sketches, collages or handwritten texts** are just a few of the ways to be artistic. As tools, you can bring along **fountain pens, ink, scissors, tape, various colored pens and painting supplies**. For example, use the following materials for the design:

*   Postcards
*   Photos from an instant camera
*   Banknotes and coins
*   Receipts and tickets
*   Newspaper clippings
*   Flyers and brochures for tourists

![Creative travel diary ideas](travel-diary.jpg)

{{< warning headline="Caution with natural materials" >}}

Even if it is very popular and seems harmless: taking **shells, corals, stones and sand** is strictly forbidden in many holiday countries such as Turkey, Egypt and Italy. From non-EU countries, you may also not import any **plant parts (e.g. leaves, flowers, seeds)** into the EU without an official [phytosanitary certificate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phytosanitary_certification). Both national nature conservation laws and international species protection regulations prohibit the export of animal, plant and geological samples. When designing your travel diary, it is better to do without these materials. Otherwise, you risk confiscation by customs and hefty fines.

{{< /warning >}} 

## Save insider tips and valuable notes for your next trip

Why did this particular experience stay so positive in your memory? **What would you do the same way again or perhaps differently?** For example, you might hike to a barely developed dream beach and notice on site that there is little shade and sharp-edged rocks lurk in the sea. For a more comfortable stay, you would therefore bring an umbrella and water shoes next time. These personal impressions and recommendations can hardly be reconstructed later and are lost when you, your friends or family members want to visit this place again.  

![Capture insider tips in the travel diary](travel-diary-sea-bay.jpg)

A structured travel diary solves exactly this problem. For example, note your visiting times, admission prices, personal insider tips and retrospective ratings:

*   **Sights and attractions**: Was the visit worth it in your view? Was it very crowded at that time of day? Could you have bought the ticket online in advance? Which means of transport would you recommend for getting there?
*   **Restaurants, cafés, bars**: How large was the selection on the menu? What did you try and how did you like it? What did you think of the service and the atmosphere of the location? Could you pay by card?
*   **Beaches, hiking trails, viewpoints**: What was the condition of the path like? Was a lot of physical fitness required? Was the nature untouched or was the place already developed for tourism? What should you not forget to pack?

This is how **your personal travel guide** is created along the way. If you or your relatives later visit the same region, a digital travel diary with a search function in particular saves you tedious research. Especially on longer trips, it also helps you keep track of things. Experiences and recommendations can be documented clearly and found again at any time later.

## Frequently asked questions about the travel diary, filling it in and templates

{{< faq "What are the best travel diary ideas for those who don't like writing?" >}}

You do not have to write several pages in your travel diary every day. Often 10 minutes in the evening or spread throughout the day is enough. Note the highlights of the day in bullet points, for example sights, activities or culinary delights. In a travel diary app, you can quickly and easily capture your mood, the weather and ratings using drop-down menus and star ratings. Your favorite photos are also uploaded in no time.

{{< /faq >}}

{{< faq "Should I keep my travel diary online or rather write it by hand?" >}}

Both have advantages and disadvantages. An analog travel diary impresses with a personal writing feeling, the creative DIY charm and independence from internet connections and battery levels. An online travel diary, on the other hand, offers more flexibility, search functions and the ability to embed photos, videos and links directly. Compared to the physical diary, memories are protected against loss and damage, you can make changes at any time and share your notes with family and friends. If you like, you can even combine both variants, for example by making handwritten notes on the go that you later transfer to your digital travel diary.

{{< /faq >}}

{{< faq "How do I manage to write in my travel diary regularly on holiday?" >}}

It is best to schedule fixed times – for example in the evening at the hotel or during transfer times on trains, buses or planes. Just a few minutes are enough to jot down bullet points on the go that you can flesh out later. This way, writing quickly becomes a [habit]({{< relref "posts/habit-tracker" >}}) without taking up valuable holiday time.
