## What is a no-code CRM?

This novel term is made up of two parts: No-Code and CRM. [No-code]({{< relref "posts/20250307-low-code" >}}) is a development approach that makes it possible to develop your own applications with ready-made modules without having to program. **CRM** means customer relationship management, i.e. the systematic organization of [customer relationships]({{< relref "pages/industry-solutions/kundenmanagement" >}}).

Accordingly, a no-code CRM is a software solution that allows you to create **workflows, data structures and apps for your customer relationship management** yourself – without having to write a single line of code. In contrast, with a low-code CRM you can still make certain additions by adding individually programmed code.

## What are the advantages of a no-code CRM?

Traditional CRM systems (such as SAP CRM or Microsoft Dynamics CRM) are often expensive, complex to set up and require IT expertise for customizations. This is exactly where no-code solutions come into play: they make it possible to create a customized, sometimes even free CRM system quickly, flexibly and without programming knowledge.
  
| Aspect | Classic CRM | No-code CRM |
| ---------------------- | ---------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Development time | weeks or months | days or hours |
| Hosting | mostly locally on own servers | mostly in the cloud |
| Scalability | slow and cumbersome | fast and convenient |
| Costs | high | low or free |
| Integration | difficult | easy |
| Customizability | unlimited, but complex | flexible and simple |
| IT dependency | very high | low |

Take a look at the advantages of no-code for CRM systems in detail:

{{< accordion "Up and running quickly" >}}
Thanks to ready-made software modules, you can reduce the development time for your no-code CRM to a minimum. Getting started is also smooth: in most cases, you can get started right away without paying and put the first version of your CRM system online in no time at all.
{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion "Flexibly customizable" >}}
No-code tools are user-friendly and easy to use, so your employees can make the desired customizations themselves and don't have to wait for developers to program the relevant functions. In addition, you can easily evaluate and visualize data without SQL queries.
{{< /accordion >}}
  
{{< accordion "Independent of IT resources" >}}
Traditional CRM systems are often complex custom-made products that consume a lot of IT resources for development and operation. No-Code makes your CRM independent of the availability of [scarce IT specialists]({{< relref "posts/it-fachkraeftemangel" >}}). Instead, your employees put their ideas into practice themselves – by means of [Citizen Development]({{< relref "posts/20250317-citizen-developer" >}}).
{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion "Scalable Cloud" >}}
As a rule, you can use a no-code CRM system in a cloud for which you do not need to purchase your own servers. In contrast to an on-premises CRM system, you can scale a [cloud solution]({{< relref "posts/cloud-computing" >}}) quickly and easily, i.e. add more functions, storage space or user accounts if your requirements, data volumes or number of employees increase.
{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion "Cost-efficient" >}}
Many no-code providers rely on a pay-as-you-go model; this means that you start with your CRM system for free and only pay for the resources (e.g. storage space) that you actually use. No-code solutions significantly reduce the development costs for your CRM and make CRM software affordable for small businesses. In addition, you do not need your own infrastructure for a CRM system in the cloud, which saves you high expenses.
{{< /accordion >}}

{{< accordion "Integrative" >}}
Most no-code solutions are highly compatible and open to the connection of other systems. With a wide range of integrations, you can bring together all customer data, sales processes, documents, appointments and support requests in a central tool, making it easier for you to carry out well-founded analyses and decisions.

## Who is a no-code CRM suitable for?

You may have heard somewhere that a no-code solution is only suitable as a CRM for small companies. But that's not true. It is a [myth]({{< relref "posts/no-code-mythen" >}}) that no-code CRM systems are only suitable for small companies and projects. The solutions are also used by SMEs and large, international companies. This is because they often struggle with traditional CRM systems, fragmented solutions and inefficient processes.
  
**Small and medium-sized companies** often have a limited [budget]({{< relref "posts/20250606-budgetplanung" >}}) and do not have their own IT department. Nevertheless, they face similar challenges to large companies: Contacts, quotes, orders and support requests need to be managed professionally. Thanks to No-Code, they can build a free CRM system without IT experts that grows with their requirements.

However, no-code tools also help **large companies** to react quickly and flexibly to changes – with the difference that they generally have even higher requirements in terms of security, governance and compliance, which is why cloud CRM is not always an option. Instead, large companies often rely on their own IT infrastructure and an on-premises CRM system in favor of [digital sovereignty]({{< relref "posts/digitale-souveraenitaet" >}}).

## No-code, on-premises and cloud: CRM systems in comparison

Below we present six CRM tools, which we classify according to the criteria **functional scope, flexibility, costs and data protection**.

### Pipedrive

{{< customer-logo image="no-code-crm-pipedrive.jpg" firma="Pipedrive Inc." branche="Cloud CRM" ort="New York, USA" >}}

**Functionality and flexibility:**
Pipedrive is a cloud CRM system that is completely specialized in sales. It plays to its strengths in visualizing sales processes and is known for its Kanban-based sales pipeline. While you can customize Pipedrive to your needs, it is functionally limited and not as flexible as a true no-code CRM. Fortunately, the platform offers modern automation and AI features (e.g. for composing emails) as well as native integrations with Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Mailchimp, Slack and other widely used business applications.  
  
**Costs and data protection:**
If you want to build a free CRM system with Pipedrive, you will be disappointed: the basic version already costs 14 euros per user per month. Furthermore, as a pure cloud CRM, Pipedrive makes an on-premises installation impossible. This is questionable for reasons of data sovereignty, as the cloud is hosted in Amazon Web Services (AWS) data centers. Although Pipedrive was founded in Estonia, its headquarters are now located in New York. The data storage for European companies is supposedly GDPR-compliant, but a data outflow to the USA cannot be ruled out.

### Salesforce

{{< customer-logo image="no-code-crm-salesforce.jpg" firma="Salesforce Inc." branche="Cloud CRM" ort="San Francisco, USA" >}}

**Functionality and flexibility:**
Salesforce is the global market leader for cloud CRM systems and is broadly positioned: The solutions cover not only CRM and sales, but also e-commerce, marketing, data analytics and customer service. The platform is characterized by high scalability and integration capability, for example via AppExchange, user-defined objects and APIs. However, it is more of a low-code CRM, as not everything can be customized without IT experts. With *Agentforce*, Salesforce offers the futuristic option of creating your own AI agents and processing customer inquiries fully automatically.  

**Costs and data protection:**
Salesforce offers a free CRM for small businesses that allows up to two users. After that, the cheapest paid version costs 25 euros per user per month. Attention, cost trap: Depending on the range of functions, prices quickly rise to several hundred euros per user and month. Salesforce is based in the USA and hosts its cloud solutions in data centers around the world. For European customers, the locations in the EU are an option, although even there US authorities can access the cloud data. Salesforce does not have an on-premises CRM system.

### Zoho CRM

{{< customer-logo image="no-code-crm-zoho.png" firma="Zoho Corporation" branche="Cloud CRM" ort="Chennai, India" >}}

**Functionality and flexibility:**
Zoho CRM offers the usual features of a CRM system for free: customer, lead, quote, contract and email management. Integrations with Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Slack and other business solutions are easily possible. From the Enterprise version upwards, you are provided with an AI assistant that can provide analyses and forecasts based on your data. Zoho CRM is part of the larger Zoho One Suite, which also includes solutions for marketing, HR, finance, projects and operations. The modular CRM system in the cloud is therefore individually customizable and expandable, but limited in its flexibility compared to a true no-code CRM.
  
**Costs and data protection:**
The free version of Zoho CRM only supports 3 users. Larger teams have to shell out at least 14 euros per user per month; the enterprise version costs 40 euros. Zoho is an Indian cloud provider that primarily stores and processes the data of European customers within the EU. Access from India is still possible. If you opt for Zoho, you must use your CRM system online. An on-premises CRM system is not available. As a customer, you are also responsible for configuring your cloud CRM in compliance with the GDPR.

### Softr

{{< customer-logo image="no-code-crm-softr.jpg" firma="Softr Platforms GmbH" branche="No-Code App Builder" ort="Berlin, Germany" >}}

**Functionality and flexibility:**
Softr is a no-code platform that allows you to build a customized CRM system app yourself by tapping into data sources such as Google Sheets or SQL databases or by creating your own databases in Softr. You can then create individual apps and frontends to visualize the data, e.g. contact lists, sales pipelines, forms, workflows and dashboards. Softr offers a very high degree of flexibility: on the one hand, you can customize your CRM system app exactly to your needs without code; on the other hand, countless other use cases can be covered beyond no-code CRM.
  
**Costs and data protection:**
The very limited free version includes an app with up to 10 users and 5,000 data records. For extended functions (e.g. Kanban, calendar), which should be available free of charge in a normal no-code CRM system, you pay between 49 and 139 US dollars per month, for the connection of SQL databases even 269 US dollars per month. Softr is headquartered in Berlin and is therefore bound by German/European data protection standards. However, it hosts its cloud mainly in AWS data centers, which does not rule out access from the USA. An on-premises version for full data security is not available.

### Stacker

{{< customer-logo image="no-code-crm-stacker.jpg" firma="Stacker Software Ltd" branche="No-Code App Builder" ort="London, England" >}}

**Functionality and flexibility:**
Similar to Softr, Stacker allows you to create your own apps from data sources such as Google Sheets without any programming knowledge. Stacker is suitable for quickly building a CRM system app, but is somewhat less spectacular in terms of functions compared to other app builders. Dashboards, sales pipelines and customer profiles are also familiar from other solutions. In principle, Stacker offers a high degree of flexibility, but it is more specialized in basic processes and CRM systems for small companies.  
  
**Costs and data protection**
Unfortunately, you cannot build a free CRM system with Stacker. The starter version costs 29 US dollars per month for an app with 5 users and 10,000 data records. Three apps with higher limits and more functions already cost 149 US dollars, the unlimited Pro version 299 US dollars per month. Depending on team size and requirements, Stacker can therefore quickly add up. The cloud platform, which is headquartered in London, primarily uses data centers outside the EU. This makes it difficult for customers to comply with the GDPR, especially when it comes to data transfer to third countries. Stacker does not offer an on-premises CRM system.

**Functionality and flexibility:**
SeaTable is a no-code CRM solution that combines data management, app building and AI automations. With SeaTable you can create a no-code CRM in 5 steps:

1.  **Define data structure**: Which processes do you want to map (e.g. acquisition, sales, support) and how many tables do you need for your data (e.g. contacts, products, deals, activities)?
2.  **Create links**: Which tables, data and processes are related to each other? For example, link companies with contacts and deals.
3.  **Set up automations**: Automate recurring routine tasks such as quote generation, follow-up e-mails and appointment reminders.
4.  **Create apps**: Use the App Builder to visualize your key figures on dashboards, your deals in a Kanban pipeline and create forms for systematic data collection.
5.  **Test and optimize**: Provide your employees with the prototype of your no-code CRM and customize the system as you wish.

{{< faq "What are the advantages of a no-code CRM?" >}}
A significantly shorter development time, fast implementation, high cost efficiency and scalability, flexible adaptability and low dependency on (unavailable) IT specialists: These advantages make no-code CRM systems equally attractive for small businesses, SMEs and large corporations.
